Cyberduck is a libre file transfer client that lets you connect to servers and cloud storage from your desktop. Whether you're uploading files to Amazon S3, managing a remote server over SFTP, or syncing folders to Google Drive, Cyberduck provides a familiar interface across macOS and Windows — plus a powerful `duck` command-line tool for Linux, macOS, and Windows.

## Getting started quickly

<Steps>
  <Step title="Download Cyberduck">
    Download Cyberduck from [cyberduck.io](https://cyberduck.io) for macOS or Windows. For the CLI, see [CLI installation](/cli/installation).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open a connection">
    Click **Open Connection** and choose your protocol. Enter your server address, username, and password (or select an SSH key or OAuth login).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Browse and transfer files">
    Navigate your remote storage like a local folder. Drag files to upload, or double-click to download. Use **Synchronize** to mirror entire directories.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save as a bookmark">
    Save your connection as a bookmark so you can reconnect with one click. Bookmarks store your host, credentials, and connection settings.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Cyberduck is open source under the GPL-3.0 license. Core libraries are also used in [Mountain Duck](https://mountainduck.io/), which mounts remote storage as a local drive.
</Note>
